Digsy

An apartment tour tracker for New York City and New Jersey renters

What Digsy Does

Digsy is a mobile app for people hunting for an apartment. It keeps a private record of every building and unit you tour, so you never lose track of what you saw or what it cost. It calculates the true net-effective rent after concessions, so you can compare apartments honestly instead of guessing. It gives you a built-in checklist of the questions worth asking on a tour, and lets you email those questions to a leasing agent and paste the reply back in. It reads the details out of a listing link, a leasing email, or a screenshot so you rarely have to type anything. It captures walkthrough videos of each unit, pins every building on a map, and compares your options side by side. Everything you enter stays on your device.

Purchases

Digsy is free to download and free to use for tracking up to three buildings, along with the checklist and the net-effective rent math. Digsy Pro is a single one-time purchase, not a subscription, that unlocks unlimited buildings, side-by-side comparison, the listing and email capture tools, PDF reports, and commute times.

  • Digsy Pro is a one-time purchase of $6.99. It is yours forever and there is no recurring charge.

The purchase is billed and managed entirely by Apple through your App Store account. Digsy never sees or stores your payment details.

Privacy Policy

Last updated July 16, 2026

This policy explains what information Digsy handles and how it is used. Digsy is built by Abonglab. In short: Digsy does not collect your data, does not require an account, and keeps everything you enter on your own device. By using Digsy you agree to the practices described here.

Information We Collect

We do not collect your personal data. Digsy has no account or login, no analytics, no advertising, and no tracking. The information you create in the app lives on your device, and Abonglab never receives it. Specifically:

  • Your tours. The buildings, units, rents, notes, checklist answers, and walkthrough videos you add are stored locally on your device using Apple's on-device database. They are never sent to Abonglab.
  • iCloud (optional). If you turn on iCloud sync in Settings, your tours are kept in step across your own Apple devices through your private iCloud account. This data is stored in your iCloud, governed by Apple's privacy policy, and Abonglab cannot read it.
  • Location. If you allow it, Digsy uses your location only while the app is in use, on your device, to suggest the building you are standing in front of and to estimate commute times. Your location is never sent to Abonglab.
  • Camera, microphone, and photos. If you use them, Digsy records walkthrough videos or imports existing ones, entirely on your device. This media never leaves your device unless you choose to share it yourself.
  • Purchases. If you buy Digsy Pro, the purchase is handled entirely by Apple through StoreKit. Abonglab never sees or stores your payment information.

Maps

Digsy uses Apple Maps to search for addresses, place pins, and estimate travel times. These requests are handled by Apple under Apple's own privacy policy. When you choose to import details from a listing link, Digsy fetches that public web page directly; no personal information is sent in the request.

How We Use Information

Because Digsy keeps your information on your device, that information is used only to run the app's features for you: tracking your tours, doing the rent math, showing your buildings on a map, and syncing across your own devices if you enable iCloud. We do not use your data for advertising and we do not sell your information to anyone.

Data Retention and Deletion

Your data stays on your device for as long as you keep the app. Deleting the app removes everything stored locally. If you enabled iCloud sync, you can remove that data from your device's iCloud settings, and it is managed by your own Apple account.

Children's Privacy

Digsy is not directed at children under thirteen, and because Digsy collects no data, we do not knowingly collect information from children.
